# Retail-Store-Sales-Analysis

A Python-based data analysis project that analyzes sales patterns of a local retail store and provides actionable business recommendations to improve revenue, customer retention, and store efficiency.

The project uses historical transaction data to identify customer behavior trends and generates suggestions using simple rule-based logic.

---

## 📌 Problem Statement

A local retail store wants to improve its sales by analyzing customer purchasing patterns.

Key observations:

- Monday has the lowest customer footfall
- Sunday has the highest sales activity
- Saturday performs below average
- Most weekday sales occur between 2 PM - 5 PM
- Morning and evening hours have lower customer activity

The objective is to analyze these trends and suggest strategies to increase sales and optimize store operations.

---

## 📊 Dataset

The dataset contains 550 transaction records of a local retail store.

Features included:

- Transaction ID
- Date
- Day
- Time
- Product Category
- Product Name
- Quantity
- Unit Price

- Payment Mode

## 🔎 Analysis Performed

### Customer Footfall Analysis
Identifies:
- Highest customer traffic day
- Lowest customer traffic day
- Daily transaction patterns

### Time-Based Analysis
Divides store hours into:

- Morning
- Afternoon
- Evening

Finds:

- Peak shopping hours
- Low customer activity periods

---

## 💡 Recommendation System

The system provides suggestions based on analysis results.

### Low Sales Day Strategy
For low-performing days:

- Reduce operational costs
- Provide employee weekly breaks
- Introduce special offers

### High Sales Day Strategy

For high-demand days:

- Maintain higher inventory
- Introduce loyalty offers
- Improve customer retention

### Low Traffic Hour Strategy

To increase sales:

- Start home delivery services
- Provide free delivery above ₹300
- Introduce combo deals and family packs
